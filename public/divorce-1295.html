Here's exactly what happens.

Built from hundreds of sessions, neuroscience research, and a genuine commitment to what actually works.

  1. 1

    A private call with each of you — before anything starts

    Ryan meets with each person separately, one-on-one. He listens for what you're afraid of, what you're hoping for, what you expect to run into — and what would personally help you feel comfortable, confident, and ready. No surprises. No ambushes going in.

  2. 2

    Four questions to get your mind ready

    Before the session, both of you receive the same four questions to reflect on. Research shows this kind of priming shifts people out of fight-or-flight and into a collaborative mindset — so you arrive oriented toward agreement, not still bracing for a fight. Most mediation practices skip this entirely and throw you straight in. We don't.

  3. 3

    Pre-session prep — done in advance, not during

    You'll complete a questionnaire covering the details the session needs: assets, debts, kids' ages, income — all of it. So when you're together, you're not sorting through paperwork. You're making decisions.

  4. 4

    One three-hour session — start to finish

    Most couples reach full agreement in a single session. You come in without an agreement. You leave with every issue resolved and written down. For most people, that's the end of the process — and many describe it as something they're genuinely proud of. Not easy. But done, and done well.
